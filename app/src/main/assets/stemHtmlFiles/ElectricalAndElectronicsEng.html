Electrical and Electronics Engineering

Electrical and Electronics Engineering is an engineering discipline that deals with the generation, transmission and distribution of power in form of electrical, hydro and natural resources such as wind and solar energy to light our cities, our homes and our industries. It is concerned with the practical applications of electricity in all its forms, including those of the field of electronics. It specifically deals with the design and application of equipment, devices and systems which use electricity, electronics and electromagnetism. Electrical and Electronics Engineers design components for electronics equipment, communication systems, power grids, automobiles, buildings, industries and more. Where can an electrical and electronics engineer work?

Where can an electrical and electronics engineer work?

  • Telecommunication systems
  • Manufacturing industries
  • Mining industry
  • Mineral and oil refinery processing industries
  • Electrical power stations
  • Constructions industries such as instilling and wiring of buildings
  • Electronic industry such as designing of household appliances and industrial appliances/li>
  • Electrical control of industrial machinery such as those in the mines, petrochemical plants and industrial engineering
  • National government departments

Like all engineering programs except for computer engineering, an electrical and electronics engineering degree is a 5-year program.
