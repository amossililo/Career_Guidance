Aeronautical Engineering

Aeronautical Engineering deals with the development of aircraft and spacecraft that includes military aircraft design and development, along with commercial airline design and satellite technology. It specifically deals with the design, analysis of aircraft structures, aircraft performances and their operations to transport goods, people and services around the globe. It is a program that prepares individuals to apply mathematical principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of aircraft, missiles, space vehicles, and their systems; applied research on flight and orbital characteristics; and the development of systems and procedures for the launching, guidance, and control of air and space vehicles.

Where can an aerospace engineer work?

  • Manufacturing companies that manufacture aircraft and spacecraft
  • Airports and airlines or airways
  • In the military, maintenance of war aircraft and space crafts
  • Government departments such as national airport corporations
  • Education and research

Like all engineering programs except for computer engineering aeronautical engineering, the degree is a 5-year program.
