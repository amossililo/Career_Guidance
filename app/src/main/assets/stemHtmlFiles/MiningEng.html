Mining Engineering is a multidisciplinary branch of engineering that deals with the development and preparation of a mine in order to safely and efficiently extract resources while protecting the environment. It involves the location of valuable and accessible material deposits and devises processes for extracting them safely from the earth for the manufacturing industry and utilities. Mining Engineers strive to extract the minerals for the least cost and the lowest environmental impact. It is a program that prepares individuals to apply mathematical principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of mineral extraction, processing and refining systems, including open pit and shaft mines, prospecting and site analysis equipment and instruments, environmental and safety systems, mine equipment and facilities, mineral processing and refining methods and systems, and logistics and communications systems. Mining Engineering encompasses a wide range of engineering disciplines such as civil engineering, mechanical engineering, electrical engineering, metallurgy, geology, finance, law and economics. Mining engineering is sometimes referred to as a junk of all trade but master of none. Some duties of a mining engineer include:

  • Designing mines, both underground and open-pits (surface mines
  • Supervising constructions of mines, tunnels and shafts Machinery production and design
  • Designing transportation plans to move minerals to processing plants
  • Writing technical reports for miners, fellow engineers and mine managers
  • Solving problems related to pollution, sustainability and land reclamation
  • Monitoring safety mining operations, especially ensuring proper ventilation to all working surfaces
  • Ensuring the safety of mining equipment and assessing mine equipment supplies
  • Establishing extraction systems
  • Reclaiming mine sites
