SCIENCE AND MATHEMATICS PROGRAMS

i. Applied Mathematics

A program that focuses on the application of mathematics and statistics to the solution of functional problems in fields such as engineering and the applied sciences. It includes instruction in natural phenomena modelling continuum mechanics, reactiondiffusion, wave propagation, dynamic systems, numerical analysis, controlled theory, asymptotic methods, variation, optimization theory, inverse problems, and applications to specific scientific and industrial topics.

ii. Computational and Applied Mathematics

A program that focuses on the application of a broad range of mathematical and computational methods to modelling, analysis, algorithm development, and simulation for the solution of complex scientific and engineering problems. It includes instruction in numerical analysis, discrete mathematics, operations research, optimization, differential equations, statistics, scientific computation, and applications to specific scientific and industrial topics.

iii. Computational Mathematics

A program that focuses on the application of mathematics to the theory, architecture, and design of computers, computational techniques, and algorithms. It includes instruction in computer theory, cybernetics, numerical analysis, algorithm development, binary structures, combinatory, advanced statistics, and related topics.

iv. Financial Mathematics

A program that focuses on the application of mathematics and statistics to the finance industry, including the development, critique, and use of various financial models. It includes instruction in probability theory, statistical analysis, numerical methods, computation and simulation methods, stochastic processes, economics, and financial markets and applications.

v. Operations Research

A program that focuses on the development and application of complex mathematical or simulation models to solve problems involving operational systems, where the system concerned is subject to human intervention. It includes instruction in advanced multivariate analysis, application of judgment and statistical tests, optimization theory and techniques, resource allocation theory, mathematical modelling, control theory, statistical analysis, and applications to specific research problems. .

vi. Mathematical Statistics and Probability

A program that focuses on the mathematical theory underlying statistical methods and their use. It includes instruction in probability theory parametric and non-parametric inference, sequential analysis, multivariate analysis, Bayesian analysis, experimental design, time series analysis, re-sampling, robust statistics, limit theory, infinite particle systems, stochastic processes, martingales, Markov processes, and Banach spaces.

vii. Mathematics and Statistics

A program with a general synthesis of mathematics and statistics or specialization that draws from mathematics and statistics. It includes instruction in calculus, linear algebra, numerical analysis and partial differential equations, discrete mathematics, probability theory, statistics, computing, and other related topics.
