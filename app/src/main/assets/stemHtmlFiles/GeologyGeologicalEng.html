Geotechnical and Geo-Environmental Engineering

A program that prepares individuals to apply geotechnical engineering methods, which deal with the analysis, design and construction of earth and earth supported structures, to the application of environmental problems, such as waste containment, waste disposal, and construction of landfills, soil permeation, soil analysis, and soil improvement. It includes instruction in soil mechanics, soil dynamics, soil behaviour, waste management and containment systems, geosynthetics, geochemistry, earth structures, geo-environmental engineering, geotechnical engineering, earthquake engineering, and foundation engineering. Studying Geotechnical engineering may find one working as:

  • Rock mechanics engineer
  • Geotechnical engineer
  • Ground control and instrumentation engineer
  • Seismic engineer
  • Sustainability consultant
  • Environmental consultant
  • Open-pit optimization engineer
  • Tunneling engineer
  • Site investigations engineer
  • Geological engineer
  • Site engineer
  • Hazard control engineer
  • Risk assessment and control engineer

Career opportunities include:

  • Mining industry
  • Railway industry
  • Construction industry in the construction of roads, dams, bridges, tunnels and industrial buildings
  • International disaster management organizations
  • Water utility and waste management companies
  • National and local government departments
  • Research and academia
  • Consultancy
