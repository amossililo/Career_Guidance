ElectroMechanical engineering

A program that prepares individuals to apply mathematical principles to the problems associated with combining mechanical components with special emphasis on manufacturing and automated processes. It includes instruction in applied mechanics, instrumentation and monitoring, machine design, automated control techniques, fluid and thermal dynamics, circuit analysis, and solid-state electronics. It is an engineering discipline that is a combination of mechanical and electrical systems. It specifically deals with the analysis, design, and maintenance of equipment and products based on the combination of electrical/electronic and mechanical systems. Products include:

  • Electric razors
  • Electric razors
  • Medical instruments
  • Industrial type machinery
  • Complex robotic systems
  • Space satellites
  • Computer monitors
