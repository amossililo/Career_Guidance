Environmental Engineering

Environmental Engineering is the discipline that deals with the development of processes and infrastructure for the supply of water, disposal of waste and the control of pollution of all kinds, protecting public health by preventing disease transmission and preserving the quality of the environment by averting contamination and degradation of air, water and land resources. It involves the treatment and distribution of drinking water (water supply system, the collection, the treatment and disposal of wastewater (wastewater treatment) and municipal solid waste management. Environmental Engineers study the environmental impact humans have on the planet, including pollution because of development and manufacturing processes. It is a program that prepares individuals to apply scientific principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of systems for controlling contained living environments and for monitoring and controlling factors in the external natural environment, including pollution control, waste and hazardous material disposal, health and safety protection, conservation, life support, and requirements for the protection of special materials and related work environments. What do environmental engineers do?

  • Design, construct and operate water supply system facilities
  • Design, construct and operate solid waste management systems and facilities
  • Design, construct and operate wastewater treatment facilities
  • Sale and marketing of environmental control equipment
  • Enforcement of environmental standards and regulations

Career Opportunities include:

  • Public sector, national and regional environmental agencies
  • Local health departments
  • Public works departments and municipal council
  • Water and sewerage utility companies
  • Construction contractors
  • Manufacturing industries
  • Mining industry
  • Consulting firms
  • University lecturers and college professors
