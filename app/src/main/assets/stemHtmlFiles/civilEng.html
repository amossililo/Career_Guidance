Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering is an engineering discipline that deals with the planning, designing, construction and maintenance of physical and naturally built environments including public

works such as highways, transit systems, bridges, tunnels, shopping malls and complexes harbours, canals, dams, airports, stadiums and sports halls, sewerage systems, water and wastewater treatment systems, pipelines, power plants, structural components of buildings and railways.

The following are the further specialized programs in civil engineering:

i.    Structural Engineering

The program prepares to apply mathematical principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of materials and systems used in building load-bearing structures for various purposes and in different civil engineering environments, including buildings, roads, rail lines, bridges, dams, conduits, offshore platforms and workstations, and other structural shells; and the analysis of structural problems such as failure, fabrication, safety, and natural hazards.

ii.    Transportation and Highway Engineering

The program prepares individuals to apply mathematical principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of total systems for the physical movement of people, materials and information, including general network design and planning, facilities planning, site evaluation, transportation management systems, needs projections and analysis, and analysis of costs.

iii.    Water Resources Engineering

The program prepares individuals to apply mathematical principles to the design, development and operational evaluation of systems for collecting, storing, moving, conserving and controlling surface- and groundwater, including water quality control, water cycle management, management of human and industrial water requirements, water delivery, and flood control.

Civil engineers are the minds behind the way our modern towns and cities work; they are highly specialized experts, master planning, budgeting and analysts of projects. Every time you leave your home, you will see results of civil engineering from roads to railways and from skyscrapers to townhouses.

Studying civil engineering may find one working as;

•    A Site Engineer

•    Contracting Civil Engineer

•    Design Civil Engineer

•    Consulting Civil Engineer

•    Structural Engineer

•    Water Engineer

•    Geotechnical Engineer

•    Building Control Surveyor

•    Sustainability Consultant

•    Fire Risk Assessor

•    Construction Manager

Civil engineers can work in the following fields:

•    The construction industry includes roads, skyscrapers and townhouses

•    City and municipal councils

•    The aerospace industry, designing jetliners and space stations

•    Shipbuilding industry

•    Sewerage systems and water systems

•    Railway industry

•    Power industry

•    Nation government departments such as RDA and NFRA

•    Education and research

•    Consultancy

Like all engineering programs, except for computer engineering, a civil engineering degree is a 5-year program.
