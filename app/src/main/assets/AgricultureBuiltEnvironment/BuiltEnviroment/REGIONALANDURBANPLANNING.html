REGIONAL AND URBAN PLANNING
Regional and urban planning is a discipline that ensures that the development of the built environment is economic, promotes social development, is aesthetically appealing and that it meets the needs of the community. The discipline equips graduates to ensure that the land is used efficiently and that buildings are located in areas where services and facilities can be provided.
CAREER OPPORTUNITIES
Public sector- work for cities and towns, and at provincial level and national level, they work in planning, land development and developing transit systems and this takes place in collaboration with civil and structural engineers, environment engineers, government officials and lawyers. They also work in getting permissions to building facilities such as cell phone towers, hydro transmission lines and extensions to airports. They also work in cases involving land use controversies.
Voluntary sector- these include industry associations, social agencies, non-profit housing groups and Community Centers.
Private sector- these include consulting and law firms, real estate firms and retail firms.
The program is offered under full time degree level at the Copperbelt University. At least a credit in English, mathematics, physical sciences and any other two options guarantees admission.
