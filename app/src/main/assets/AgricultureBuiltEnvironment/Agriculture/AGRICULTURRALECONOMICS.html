AGRICULTURRAL ECONOMICS

Agricultural economics is the discipline that deals with the applied social sciences that deals with how producers, consumers and societies use scarce resources in the production, marketing, and consumption of food and fiber products. It is the study of maximizing profitability in agriculture to the benefit of the society. It is the application of economics principles to agriculture. Agricultural economics can be traced back to ancient Egypt, arguably to the first agricultural economist Joseph who predicted 7 years of bumper harvest and another 7 years of drought. Studying agricultural economics may find one working as;

  • Agricultural development officer
  • Agricultural appraiser
  • Agricultural commodity broker
  • Agricultural loan officer
  • Agribusiness consultant
  • Agricultural economists
  • Market researcher
  • Production agriculture manager
  • Cooperative extension agent
