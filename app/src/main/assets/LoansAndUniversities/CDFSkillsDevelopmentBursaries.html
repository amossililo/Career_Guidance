CDF Skills Development Bursaries

Skills Development Bursary

Skills Development Bursaries are used to provide for vulnerable learners to access Skills Training Institutions offering technical education, vocational and entrepreneurship programs. The aim of the
bursary is to enhance human development and drive the entrepreneurship agenda at the local
level.

The following eligibility criteria shall apply for Skills Development:
a) A Person must be a Zambian citizen with a Green National Registration Card resident in the
Constituency;
b) The applicant must be a resident of the constituency for more than 6 months;
c) The applicant must be in possession of an admission or acceptance letter from any of the
public TEVET institutions or the Zambia National Service;
d) The applicant must be aged between 16 and below 35 years; and
e) Entrepreneurs out of school that need up-skilling and re-skilling.

The following application procedure shall apply:

a) Bursary application forms shall be obtained free of charge from the Local Authority, TEVET Offices or any other Public Institution in the District and the Local Authority Website;

b) Application forms shall be typed or filed in a clear handwriting;

c) Applications to the CDFC shall be accompanied by the following documents:

i. Signed Minutes of the Ward Development Executive Committee Meeting;

ii. A signed application forms by the Ward Development Committee Chairperson. Where the

Chairperson is not available, the Vice Chairperson shall sign and where the Vice Chairperson is not available, the Committee shall nominate a member amongst themselves who shall sign the applications.

Skills Training Bursary Application

The application for Skills Training Bursary shall be submitted together with an admission or acceptance letter from any TEVETA accredited public or private institution or the Zambia National Service. Any of the following certified documents shall accompany the application:

a) Disability card or letter from the hospital denoting disability;

b) Recommendation letters from the Church/Chief/Headman/Head Teacher of previous school; and

c) Where applicable, the applicant must be in possession of a full Grade 12 School Certificate or GCE with Five (5) O-levels or equivalent or Grade 9 or 7 Certificates.

Note: School drop outs and those that have never been to school are prioritized. Persons living with disability are prioritized.
