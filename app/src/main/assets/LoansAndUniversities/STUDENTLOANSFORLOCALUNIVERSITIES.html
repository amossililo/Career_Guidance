STUDENT LOANS FOR LOCAL UNIVERSITIES

Currently student loans are given to undergraduate students joining the University of Zambia(UNZA), Copperbelt University(CBU), Kapasa Makasa University(KMU), Mukuba University(MKU), Mulungushi University(MU), Chalimbana University(CHAU) and Kwame NKrumah University from secondary schools across the country. The loans cater for tuition and accommodation and meal fees, book and project allowances. The students are required to repay the loans one year after leaving their institutions of learning. The following are universities that the higher education loans and scholarship board sponsor students to.

1. The Copperbelt University

2. The University Of Zambia

3. Kapasa Makasa University

4. Mulungushi University

5. Mukuba University

6. Chalimbana University

7. Kwame Nkrumah

ELIGIBILITY

1. Zambian with Green National Registration Cards(NRC)

2. School leaver admitted to one of the universities mentioned above

3. Should be 24 years old or below.

Method of Application

Applicants should submit the following;

1. An application letter responding to the advertisement

2. A photocopy of the admission letter from the above named university

3. A photocopy of grade 12 statement of results or a certificate

4. A photocopy of a student’s NRC

5. A photocopy of candidates parent’s or guardian’s NRC

6. Two recent passport size photos of the student with their names written at the back

7. An application form bought from any NATSAVE branch

8. A clear copy of receipt from the bank.

REPAYING THE LOAN

The higher education loans and scholarship board aim is not just to recover all loans with interest, but to raise sufficient funds to meet the rising loan demand in Zambia among the vulnerable, privileges and less privileged to live their dream. The loan recoveries also help HELSB achieve the concept of revolving funds and this enhances the loan payments to students.

REPAYMENT METHOD

A beneficiary’s loan repayment starts within one year of completion of studies or within such a period as the board decides to recall the loan whichever is earlier. However, a beneficiary can make voluntary payments before or after their studies to reduce the loan balance.

Monthly Deduction Method (MDM)

A beneficiary is expected to pay back the loan in monthly installments at a compound interest rate of 15%.

Offset Method (OM)

A beneficiary is expected to pay back the loan in huge installments or clear the whole balance at a simple interest rate of 15% on each payment.

Terms of Recoveries.

After one year grace period elapses, a beneficiary is expected to visit HELSB offices for a repayment plan.
