UNIVERSITIES

University of Zambia

The University of Zambia is Zambia’s largest university, founded in 1966. It has a student population of about 24,834. The University of Zambia has over 157 degree and postgraduate degree programmes. The University of Zambia is divided into the following faculties: School of Agricultural Sciences School of Engineering; School of Education; School of Humanities and Social Sciences;

School of Law; School of Mines; School of Medicine; School of Natural Sciences; School of

Veterinary Medicine.

Website; https://www.unza.zm/

Copperbelt University

Copperbelt University is a university in Kitwe, Zambia and takes pride in serving as a

place for high-quality education and training, enabling graduates to act efficiently and effectively in a broad range of civic and professional functions and activities. The following schools comprise the university: School of Engineering; School of Mathematics and Natural Sciences; School of Natural Resources; School of Business; School of Built Environment; Centre for Lifelong Education (CLLE); School of Medicine; School of Mines and Mineral Sciences; Dag Hammarskjold Chair for Peace, Human Rights and Conflict Management.

Website; https://www.cbu.ac.zm/

Mulungushi University

Mulungushi University (MU) comprises two campuses; the Great North Road Campus located 26 kilometers North of Kabwe, on the banks of Mulungushi River and the Kabwe Town Campus located along Mubanga Road, off Munkoyo Street in the heart of Kabwe town. Established on 1st January 2008, Mulungushi University is a multi-level curriculum university offering a wide range of degrees, diploma and certificate programmes in its nine academic faculties. The following schools comprise the university; school of agriculture and natural resources, school of business studies, school of social sciences, school of science, engineering and technology,

school of education, school of medicine and health sciences;

Website; https://www.mu.ac.zm/

Northrise University

Northrise University’s goal is to educate Zambia’s future leaders to

help an impoverished country evolve into a self-sufficient nation. At the cornerstone of a Northrise education rests the notion that graduates remain in their country to go on to influence the lives of other Zambians. Northrise University has local and international students who receive training in academic disciplines at undergraduate and postgraduate levels. Northrise is the first University in Zambia to offer online programs.Northrise University offers eleven undergraduate and graduate programs from several disciplines: Business, Information and Communication Technologies, Theology, Law, Health and Social Sciences, and Education.

The University of Lusaka

The University of Lusaka (UNILUS) is a modern private university established under the

University Act No. 11 of 1999 repealed as the Higher Education Act No. 4 of 2013. The

University commenced its operations in 2007, initially as Institute of Corporate Training and

Research (ICTAR) and served as sorrage of the Masters Degree programmes offered by the

Copperbelt University.

In 2008, the University of Lusaka introduced its Bachelor’s Degree programmes in

partnership with the Copperbelt University and in 2009 began offering its own programmes.

Following this auspicious beginning, the University now offers a wide-range of programmes

at Bachelor’s, Master’s and Doctoral levels.

Today, the total number of UNILUS students has risen to over 7,000 from 71 students in

2007 when the University opened. The university comprises the following schools; school of

law, school of medicine and health sciences. School of education, social sciences and

technology, school of business studies. The university comprises the following faculties;

faculty of premedical sciences, faculty of medicine, faculty of public health, faculty of health

sciences, faculty of medical Radiation sciences, faculty of nursing and midwifery sciences,

faculty of pharmacy, nutrition and dietetics.

The Levy Mwanawasa Medical University (LMMU)

The Levy Mwanawasa Medical University (LMMU) was established as a Public University on 22nd May, 2018 under provisions of Section 132 of the Higher Education Act, No. 4 of 2013, by way of Statutory Instrument No. 39 and 40 of 2018. At its establishment, the LMMU became the fourth Public University and is the largest purpose-built health training institution in Zambia offering various education and training programmes for health professionals.

The LMMU national hub is a 3,000-student capacity campus including a 1,000 capacity National Health Reference Library, and 200 bed National Skills Laboratory Complex. The Chainama College of Health Sciences, Dental Training School, Levy Mwanawasa University Teaching Hospital and Chainama Hills College Hospital have been integrated into the LMMU. The LMMU has 10 provincial training hubs and multiple satellite practicum sites constituting its nationwide network of academic health complexes.The university is situated approximately 10 Kilometers East of Lusaka on the Great East road in Chainama Area. The University is affiliated to and recoginised by the follwoing: the university comprises of; school of medicine and clinical sciences, school of public health and environmental sciences, school of health sciences, school of nursing.

Website; http://lmmu.ac.zm/

Eden University

Eden University is a privately owned University whose main campus is located in Barlastone Park area in Lusaka, which is the capital city of Zambia.

Eden University has three other campuses namely; the Great East Road campus located directly opposite the University of Zambia main entrance, the Mansa Campus located in Luapula Province, Chipata Campus located in Eastern Province and a marketing and sales office in Kitwe located at Mega Complex, off Puma filling station along Independence Avenue.

The University was founded in 2001 as an Institute which offered several courses under TEVETA and was accredited to City and Guilds, London Chamber of Commerce and

Association of Business Executives.

In 2010 Eden Institute was affiliated to Rusangu University and the University of Zambia to offer courses in; Diploma in Primary Teachers Education and in 2013 the Institute was registered with the Ministry of Education and the Health Professions Council of Zambia to offer diplomas.

Eden Institute was officially registered as a University in 2016 with the Higher Education Authority under Act number 4 of 2013 and has since been fully operational with five schools currently offering a wide range of diploma and degree programmes.

The Schools:

● School Of Education, Humanities and Social Sciences

● School Of Medicine and Health Sciences

● School Of Pharmacy

● School Of Natural Sciences

● School of Nursing and Midwifery Sciences

● School Of Law and Business Studies

University website; https://www.edenuniversity.edu.zm/
